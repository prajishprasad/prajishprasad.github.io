Hemangee Kapoor - DEI Activities

Diversity - One is a minority and faces challenges - One is a majority and has empathy - Organisation

Diversity - encourage differences in any sense Equity - fairness Inclusion - creating an environment where diversity thrives Interesting comment by Mrityunjay about how diversity can be enforced top-down (e.g. organization creates policies for a more diverse workplace in recruitment). But for an inclusive environment, we have to work bottom-up - just enforcing policies will not do, but an inclusive environment is created by making sure that individuals and teams create a more inclusive environment at an individual and group level.

ACM DIE committee was formed - to identify and coordinate activities in creating a more diverse and inclusive environment, and to foster adoption of best practices.

  • Monitor diversity among different people - members, keynote speakers, conference participation

Learning from IITM’s Data Science Program

Why create such a program

Only 8-10 lakhs out of 84 lakhs join engineering. Not a large number - most students join a BSc, BA, BCom

Issues - extreme competition - High cost - quality - private institutions - Low employability - High drop off

What IITM does - - Large scale - High quality - Any background Premise - “Anybody can learn to code” GATE, JEE - goal is to eliminate students NPTEL - 2400+ courses - goal - provide quality education at scale

Questions/Issues - - How to assuredly produce employable skilled graduates at a larger scale? - Faculty time - Keep fees affordable - Accessible to all who might be interested – may not have a strong background - Protect the brand of institutions

How did we create such a program

(Refer Slide) Hire instructors who run the course - faculty oversee Evaluations - equal (if not, more) rigorous CGPA - Nice bell curve Social impact

AI Assisted Programming - Sriram Rajamani

Two paradigms - Rule based (logical rules) and ML Models. Problems are becoming a mix of both paradigms. E.g. - Windows - Search Engine?? Can both paradigms coexist? Express domain knowledge as rules. Training data is available as well.

Case Study 1 - Heterogenous Data Extraction

Formed documents - e.g. - Trip documents - created by machines, consumed by humans - heterogenous format. Formats can change. Goal: Predictable and robust extraction. Use ML models along with domain specific language rules

Case Study 2 -

CoPilot - Large gap between what code generates and what it understands. Augment large language models with guard rules Jigsaw vision (ICSE 2022) - Pre-processing, post-processing, transformations (Learning from feedback)

Pre-processing - Prompt the large language model Post-processing - Do automatic transformations based on output of tests - e.g. Variable name transformation, argument transformation, AST to AST transformation - Get learner feedback - feed it back into the model

Question - Implications for teachers - Build variants/on top of CoPilot - Students are prompted - one incorrect attempt - give hints - don’t give them the answer Maybe not teach code at all!
